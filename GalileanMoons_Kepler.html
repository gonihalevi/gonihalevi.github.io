$$P^2 = \frac{4\pi^2}{GM_\mathrm{Jup}} a^3$$$$\log{(P^2)} = \log{\left(\frac{4\pi^2}{GM_\mathrm{Jup}}\right)} + \log{(a^3)}$$$$2 \log{(P)} = \log{\left(\frac{4\pi^2}{GM_\mathrm{Jup}}\right)} + 3\log{(a)}$$$$\log{(P)} = \frac{1}{2} \log{\left(\frac{4\pi^2}{GM_\mathrm{Jup}}\right)} + \frac{3}{2}\log{(a)}$$

Comparing this to the form for a line, $y = mx+b$, we see that the slope, $m$, is $3/2$ (as we found for our best-fit) and the y-intercept, $b$, is $\frac{1}{2} \log{\left(\frac{4\pi^2}{GM_\mathrm{Jup}}\right)}$. $$ 2b = \log{(4\pi^2)}-\log{(GM_\mathrm{Jup})}$$ $$ \log{(GM_\mathrm{Jup})} = \log{(4\pi^2)}-2 b$$ $$ GM_\mathrm{Jup} = 10^{\log{(4\pi^2)}-2 b}$$ $$ M_\mathrm{Jup} = 10^{\log{(4\pi^2)}-2 b}/G$$
